Glimmers

An Expressive Arts Journey Through Moments of Light

What is a Glimmer? A glimmer is a micro-moment of joy, safety, or connection that helps regulate your nervous system. Unlike triggers that activate distress, glimmers activate feelings of calm, peace, and wellbeing. They're the small moments that make you feel more alive, more present, more yourself.

Understanding Glimmers

The Science of Glimmers

Coined by Deb Dana, glimmers are the opposite of triggers. While triggers signal danger and activate our threat response, glimmers signal safety and activate our social engagement system through the vagus nerve.

Neurological

Activates the ventral vagal pathway, promoting feelings of safety and social connection

Physical

Softens breath, relaxes muscles, lowers heart rate, and releases feel-good neurotransmitters

Healing

Builds resilience by training your nervous system to recognize and seek out safety cues
